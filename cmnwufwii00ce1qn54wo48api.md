When I started learning programming, I thought I just needed to “learn fast”.

But over time, I realized I was making small mistakes that were slowing me down.

Here are a few things I’ve learned.

* * *

## ⚠️ 1. Focusing Too Much on Syntax

At first, I focused a lot on:

*   remembering syntax
    
*   writing code perfectly
    

But I ignored one important thing:

> Understanding the logic.

Now I realize, logic matters more than syntax.

* * *

## ⏳ 2. Expecting Fast Results

I wanted to understand everything quickly.

If something didn’t make sense immediately, I felt like I was doing something wrong.

But learning takes time.

* * *

## 🔁 3. Not Practicing Enough

Watching tutorials felt productive.

But when I tried writing code myself, I struggled.

That’s when I understood:

> Practice is where real learning happens.

* * *

## 🤔 4. Comparing Myself with Others

Seeing others solving problems faster made me feel behind.

But everyone learns at a different pace.

Comparing only made things worse.

* * *

## 🌱 5. Not Being Patient with Myself

I used to get frustrated easily.

If I didn’t understand something, I felt like quitting.

Now I’m trying to be more patient.

* * *

## 🔚 One Thing I’m Changing

Instead of rushing, I’m focusing on:

*   understanding
    
*   practicing
    
*   being consistent
    

* * *

**This is my journey. And I’m improving step by step. 🚀**

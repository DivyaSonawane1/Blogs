Everyone kept saying:

> “DSA is important.”

But no one told me *how to start*.

So I avoided it.

* * *

## 🤯 The Fear Around DSA

When I first heard about DSA, it already sounded difficult.

Algorithms. Time complexity. Problems I couldn’t even understand.

It felt like something only “smart students” could do.

And honestly, I didn’t feel like I was one of them.

* * *

## 🏃‍♀️ So I Took the Easy Way

When someone told me:

> “You don’t need DSA for Data Analytics.”

I didn’t question it.

I just switched.

Because it felt easier. Less pressure. Less confusion.

At that time, that’s exactly what I wanted.

* * *

## ⚠️ But Something Felt Off

Even after switching, I wasn’t fully satisfied.

It felt like I was avoiding something, not choosing something.

And that feeling stayed.

* * *

## 💭 What I Realized

DSA wasn’t the real problem.

**The problem was that I didn’t understand it.**

And when you don’t understand something, it automatically feels scary.

* * *

## 🌱 Where I Am Now

I’m still not great at DSA.

I still get stuck. I still feel confused sometimes.

But now, I don’t run away from it.

I’m trying to face it — slowly.

* * *

## 💬 If You Feel the Same

If DSA feels hard to you… if you’ve avoided it… if you feel like you’re behind…

You’re not alone.

* * *

## 🔚 One Small Thought

Maybe the goal isn’t to master everything instantly.

Maybe the goal is just to:

> **Start, even if you don’t fully understand.**

* * *

**This is my journey. And I’m still figuring things out. 🚀**

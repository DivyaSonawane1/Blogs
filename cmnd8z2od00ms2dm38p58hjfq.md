When I started Computer Engineering, I thought things would be clear.

They were not.

Everyone around me seemed to know what they were doing — some were doing DSA, some were building projects, some were already talking about internships.

And I was just… confused 😅

* * *

## 💻 Where I Started

Like most students, I began with **C and C++**.

At first, it felt okay — basic programs, loops, patterns.

But when things started getting deeper, I wasn’t always sure if I truly understood what I was doing.

Still, I kept going.

* * *

## 🤔 Then Came Confusion

At one point, someone told me:

> “Data Analytics is easier. You don’t need DSA.”

And honestly… that sounded perfect 😅

So I switched.

I started exploring Data Analytics, thinking maybe this is the path for me.

Something that feels less overwhelming.

But deep down, I wasn’t fully convinced.

* * *

## ⚠️ The Problem No One Talks About

The hardest part wasn’t coding.

**It was not knowing if I was on the right path.**

*   Should I focus on DSA?
    
*   Should I avoid it?
    
*   Should I follow what others are doing?
    

Every option felt right and wrong at the same time.

* * *

## 🚀 Where I Am Now

After trying different things, I’ve started learning **Java**.

Not because someone said it’s easy.

But because **I’m actually interested in it.**

And for the first time, things feel a little more clear.

Not perfect. Not easy.

But at least it feels like *my choice*.

* * *

## 🌱 What I’ve Realized (So Far)

I’m still at the beginning.

I don’t have everything figured out.

But I’ve understood one thing:

> **You don’t need to start perfectly. You just need to start somewhere.**

Trying different things is not a waste of time.

It’s part of the process.

* * *

## 💬 If You Feel the Same

If you’ve ever felt confused, switched paths, or felt like you’re behind…

You’re not alone.

* * *

## 🔚 This Is Just the Start

I’m not writing this as an expert.

I’m writing this as a student who is still figuring things out.

And maybe that’s okay.

* * *

**This is my journey. And this is just the beginning. 🚀**
